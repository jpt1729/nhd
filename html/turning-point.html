Turning Point

   ​​​​​​​At the time, many industries benefited from real time communication between hemispheres, particularly railways, newspapers, and finance. Industries like the cotton industry saw unprecedented growth. Now with instant communication, it enabled future contracts and allowed merchants to hedge cotton and secure funds. The telegram also allowed growers to remove the middleman and their commission fees. All of this allowed the South to grow economically after the Civil War and helped build new industries in states like North Carolina.

Engraving of James Buchanan, 1857, J.C. Buttre.

   The advent of the telegraph cable sparked a societal transformation of monumental proportions. Its impact extended far beyond the boardrooms of commerce, permeating the very fabric of everyday life. Families, separated by vast distances, found solace in the newfound ability to exchange messages with unprecedented speed, bridging geographical chasms and nurturing familial bonds across continents. Similarly, the telegraph's swift dissemination of news revolutionized the media landscape, democratizing access to information and catalyzing the rapid spread of ideas. Thus, the telegraph cable emerged not only as a conduit for economic growth but also as a catalyst for profound social change, ushering in an era of connectivity that would forever alter the course of history.
