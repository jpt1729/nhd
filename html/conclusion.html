Conclusion

   The transatlantic telegraph cable revolutionized communication between the Old World and the New World, which in turn transformed the American and British economies. When the cable was established, the world took its first step towards instant communication. Not only did it help the economy at the time, but it also paved the way for our modern day underwater internet cables. Today, we enjoy the privilege of underwater cables which allow us to connect with family, friends, and strangers anywhere in the world. So while the cable was decommissioned in 1965, it set the stepping stones for a second turning point in history, the internet. ​​​​​​​
