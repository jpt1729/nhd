Thesis

   In July of 1866, the third transatlantic telegraph cable was laid by Cyrus W. Field. Before 1866, transatlantic communication took weeks. In 1844, Field tried his hand at creating the first transatlantic telegraph cable. It failed after three weeks. Then 11 years later, he tried his hand again to build the second transatlantic telegraph cable. It failed after the cable broke halfway across the Atlantic. After two previous failures to complete the task, his company's third attempt made the first successful transatlantic telegraph cable. Each of his previous failures taught him how to improve on the next cable. When creating the first transatlantic telegraph cable, Cyrus W. Field's persistence transformed communication between the Old World and the New World changing the global economy forever.
